SKILLAGENTS AI — IDEAL CUSTOMER PROFILES (ICP)
================================================

ICP A: "Enterprise Digital Transformer"
-----------------------------------------
FIRMOGRAPHICS
- Company size: 1,000–10,000 employees
- Revenue: $500M–$5B ARR
- Type: Fortune 1000, publicly traded or PE-backed
- Industry: Financial services, healthcare, insurance, manufacturing, professional services
- Geography: North America (primary), UK/EU (secondary)

TECHNOLOGY SIGNALS
- Using legacy LMS: Cornerstone OnDemand, SAP SuccessFactors, or Saba
- Microsoft 365 / Teams as primary collaboration stack
- Active Workday or SAP HR deployment
- Running AI pilots with tools like Copilot for M365, GitHub Copilot, or custom LLMs

ORGANIZATIONAL SIGNALS (BUYING TRIGGERS)
- CHRO or CLO hired or promoted within the last 18 months — new leaders want quick wins
- Company has publicly announced an "AI transformation," "digital transformation," or "Future of Work" initiative
- L&D team is posting 3+ open roles (signal: they're scaling but overwhelmed)
- Annual L&D budget exceeds $500K/year
- Company completed a merger or acquisition in the last 12 months (need to train merged orgs)

PAIN POINTS WE SOLVE
- Legacy LMS has 20–30% completion rates; employees ignore it
- No visibility into which teams are AI-ready vs. falling behind
- IT is overwhelmed rolling out AI tools with no training support
- Compliance training is manual, audit trails are messy
- New AI tools adopted in pockets — no org-wide consistency

CHAMPION PERSONA
- Title: Chief Learning Officer (CLO), VP of Learning & Development, Head of Talent Development
- Secondary champion: Chief People Officer (CPO), CHRO
- Economic buyer: CFO or COO (ties to productivity ROI)
- Influencer: CTO or CIO (AI tool rollout owner)

DISQUALIFIERS
- Fewer than 1,000 employees (too small for enterprise motion)
- L&D budget under $200K
- Already 12+ months into a competing platform with a 3-year contract
- Custom-built internal LMS (signals strong internal capability / not open to vendors)

EXAMPLE FIT SCORE: 9/10 — GlobalBank Corp, NovaMed Health

---

ICP B: "Scaling Tech Company"
--------------------------------
FIRMOGRAPHICS
- Company size: 200–800 employees
- Stage: Series C or later, or profitable bootstrapped
- Revenue: $20M–$200M ARR
- Type: B2B SaaS, developer tools, fintech, data infrastructure
- Geography: North America (primary)

TECHNOLOGY SIGNALS
- No formal LMS in place (using scattered Notion docs, Loom videos, Google Drive)
- Slack-first culture (training delivery via Slack is a major win here)
- High concentration of engineers, data scientists, or product managers
- Adopting AI tools fast: Cursor, GitHub Copilot, Claude, ChatGPT for work
- Engineering team grew 50%+ in the last 12 months

ORGANIZATIONAL SIGNALS (BUYING TRIGGERS)
- Head of People or HR Business Partner hired in the last 6 months (first formal people function)
- CTO or CEO has posted on LinkedIn about "AI readiness" or "upskilling the team"
- Company raised a new funding round (budget unlocked, scaling pressure)
- Onboarding new hires is slow and inconsistent (pain is acute and felt by all managers)
- Recent all-hands or blog post mentioning "we need to be an AI-native team"

PAIN POINTS WE SOLVE
- Onboarding takes 3–6 months; new hires are unproductive too long
- No consistent way to train the team on internal tools and AI workflows
- Engineering managers spend 20% of their time on ad-hoc training instead of building
- Knowledge lives in Slack threads and tribal memory — not captured or transferable
- No way to measure whether AI tool investments are actually being used

CHAMPION PERSONA
- Title: Head of People, VP of Engineering, CTO
- Economic buyer: CEO or CFO (small company, decisions are top-down)
- Influencer: Head of Engineering or VP of Product

DISQUALIFIERS
- Fewer than 150 employees (not enough scale for ROI)
- Pre-Series C (budget uncertain, long sales cycle risk)
- Consumer-facing company with no internal tech team to train
- Engineering-only company with no non-technical roles (narrow use case)

EXAMPLE FIT SCORE: 9/10 — BuildFast SaaS
